Abai Kunanbayev

Abai Kunanbayev was born on July 29 (August 10), 1845 in the Zhidebai tract in the Chingiz Mountains, which at that time was administratively part of the Biysk district of the Tomsk province (now Abaysky district of the Abay region of the Republic of Kazakhstan), in the family of a large bai Kunanbai Oskenbayev (Uskenbayev) of the Tobykty family. Abai's family belonged to the local nobility; grandfather (Oskenbai) and great-grandfather (Irgizbai) dominated their family as rulers and biys. The home schooling started in childhood with the mullah was continued in the madrasah, with Mullah Akhmet-Riza, in Semipalatinsk, where Arabic, Persian and other Oriental languages were taught. At the same time he attended a Russian school. By the end of his studies, he began writing poetry, first attributing their authorship to his friend Kokpai Dzhantasov. From the age of 13, Kunanbai's father began to accustom Abai to the activities of the head of the family. At the age of 28, Abai moved away from her, completely engaged in self-education, but only by the age of 40 he created his first adult poems. A significant event for Abai was his communication with political exiles Evgeny Michaelis, Nifont Dolgopolov, Severin Gross. Poets and scientists of the East who adhered to humanistic ideas (Firdousi, Alisher Navoi, Nizami, Fuzuli, Ibn Sina and others), as well as works of Russian classics, and through them European literature in general, influenced the formation of Abai's worldview. He translated Krylov, Lermontov, Pushkin, Goethe and Byron. The story of the poem "Karangy tunde tau kalgyp" ("Mountains are asleep in the dark night"), which became a folk song, is characteristic. Goethe wrote "Wanderers Nachtlied" ("The Night Song of the Wanderer"), Lermontov translated it into Russian ("Mountain peaks sleep in the darkness of the night ..."), and half a century later Abai Kunanbayev transmitted its contents in Kazakh. Abai Kunanbayev promoted the spread of Russian and European culture among Kazakhs.

Origin and family

The father of Kunanbai Kazhy — agha is the sultan of the entire Karkarala outer district, later the volost administrator of the Kokshe-Tobykta volost, years of life 1804-1886.

Ulzhan's mother is from the Karakeri tribe of Naiman.

Grandfather Oskembai is the son of Yrgyzbai, the biy of the Tobykty family, years of life about 1778-1850.

Tokbal's grandmother is the daughter of Bektemyr Sultan, nicknamed Zere in marriage, from the Matai clan of the Naiman tribe. She taught Abai her native language.

Izgutty Kunanbayev — Abai's older brother is also a volost ruler.

Akylbay, the eldest son (1861-1904). Born when Abai was only 16 years old, he was brought up by Kunanbai's younger wife, Nurganym.

Abdirahman, (nicknamed "Abish" by his father) (1868-1895). He graduated from a real school in the city of Tyumen, then the Mikhailovsky School of Artillery in St. Petersburg. However, his health did not allow him to enter the Military Academy. He died of tuberculosis in 1895.

Magauia, (nicknamed "Magash" by his father) (1870-1904). After studying in Semipalatinsk, he returned to his village. At the insistence of his father, he wrote poems. His most voluminous work is the poem "Medgat-Kasym". In the last years of his life, he was a local judge.

Turagul, the youngest son (1875-1934). Public figure of the Alash party, poet, and writer. According to his own statement, he translated into Kazakh the works of Jack London "Eskimo Kish" and "Martin Eden" (translations have not been preserved), M. Gorky "Chelkash", A. S. Neverov "Marya the Bolshevik", Boleslav Prus "Antek" and others. He died in 1934 in Shymkent.

Philately and bonistics

A number of postage stamps and other philatelic materials issued in the USSR, Kazakhstan and other countries are dedicated to the memory of the Kazakh poet. In 1993, the image of Abai was placed on a banknote of the National Bank of the Republic of Kazakhstan with a nominal value of 20 tenge.
